Medical Record Technician (Release of Information)
Department of Veterans Affairs - Salisbury, NC
The primary purpose of this position is to analyze for validity, process efficiently and respond properly all requests for release of information and protecting the integrity of the data which includes medico-legal, authorized representatives, physicians,attorneys, Federal and State agencies and all other authorized third parties under the authority provided by the Privacy Act of 1974 and the Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act (HIPAA) of 1996. The incumbent shall function as technical assistant to the Release of Information Unit, (ROI) in the Health Information Management Section of the W.G. "Bill" Hefner VAMC to ensure compliance to VHA federal guidelines regarding confidentiality in the system of records.
  • Processes and sorts daily requests via mail, faxes or walk-ins from non-VA hospitals, clinics, physicians offices, insurance companies, attorneys, subpoenas (copy to ROI supervisor),court orders, research agencies, VA contract agencies, State Disability Determination Services, police reports, employee medical records, Non-VA research studies who request medical information for various reasons, and other requestors as assigned.
  • Incumbent must verify appropriate authorizations that cover and include sensitive information contained in the medical record. Authorization to release must follow the federal guidelines 38 U.S.C.4132, 42 CFR 2.1-2.67 (52 FR 21796). The incumbent is responsible for receipt of proper payment for information being released and assures that information is released according to Veterans Health Administration Handbook 1605.1 entitled "Privacy and Release of Information"guidelines.
  • Any unusual ROI requests must be discussed with the ROI Supervisor to assure a professional and ethical response.
  • The incumbent must make daily independent decisions based on acquired knowledge and ability to interpret various release of information consents as they apply to VHA Handbook 1605.1"Privacy and Release of Information." Unusual circumstances or cases will be discussed with the ROI Supervisor to assure full comprehension of VHA guidelines and Public Law as it applies to each request. Completes disclosure actions or acknowledges requests within twenty-working days of receipt (excludes weekends and Federal holidays), the time standard which requires monitoring and maintaining requests with sufficient information so as to reflect current status and pending action.
  • Arranges appointments for individuals to review their own medical records in the incumbent's presence or in the presence of the ROI Supervisor when appropriate to protect the integrity of the medical record.
  • The incumbent is responsible for accurate daily entry of ROI requests in the "Release of Information Management" DSS/ROI Manager's software on a daily basis for the purpose of maintaining accurate statistics for preparation for the Annual Report of Compliance with the Freedom of Information Act (FOIA) (RCN 72-0408).
  • Every request must be date stamped and reviewed to determine whether the request for access should be granted or denied. Conducts extensive searches for information and reports not initially located within the medical charts,and searches to properly identify the correct patient on requests that do not contain pertinent or adequate information essential for retrieving the records,i.e. social security number (SSN), proper name spelling, date of birth, etc.
  • In the performance of the above duties, the incumbent has daily access to print out specific data from the DSS/ROI Manager Software to meet the request for medical records via the electronic files Computerized Patient Record System (CPRS) containing sensitive data, which has been protected under the provisions of the Privacy Act of 1974,FOIA, HIPAA, and VHA Handbook 1605.1 in order to perform the incumbent's duties.
  • In addition, the incumbent must state the requested information being requested, if denied a reason needs to be entered under comments and details explaining unusual circumstances to support external customer support.
